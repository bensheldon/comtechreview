You can use this form to find text references in the Community Technology Review. You can find specific words or combinations of words. The text search engine will provide what it thinks is a "weighted list" of matching documents. The best matches are shown first; with a link to the matching article. If you want to narrow your search, you can use standard Boolean expressions (ie using keywords AND, OR, and NOT, and grouped with parentheses).

Using Special Search Terms

The text search engine allows queries to be formed from arbitrary Boolean expressions containing the keywords AND, OR, and NOT, and grouped with parentheses. For example:

  1. community networking

    finds documents containing 'community' or 'networking'

  2. community or networking

    same as above

  3. community and networking

    finds documents containing both 'community' and 'networking'

  4. community not networking

    finds documents containing 'community' but not 'networking'

  5. (community not networking) and rural

    finds documents containing 'rural', plus 'community' but not 'networking'

  6. net*

    finds documents containing words starting with 'net'
