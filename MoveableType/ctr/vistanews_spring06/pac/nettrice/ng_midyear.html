CTC VISTAs - Digital Media with Youth

By Nettrice R. Gaskins, Digital Media With Youth Priority-Area Coordinator

This year more than fifteen CTC VISTAs were placed with community media and technology programs to help young people gain digital media skills that prepare them for college and tomorrow's workforce. Digital media encompasses digital video, digital audio, the World Wide Web, other technologies and applications that can be used to create and distribute digital content.  However, it is important for new CTC VISTAs not be distracted by just learning new tools and applications but to also understand the process of teaching youth to make digital media. During their mid-year reflections, many CTC VISTAs reported that the priority-area web site, a collection of digital media/youth-related resources, and the bulletin board were very helpful in this regard.

The challenges expressed by the CTC VISTAs in the digital media for youth area in their mid-year reflections were not surprising and often are the challenges faced by youth workers in non-profit environments:

  • ever-increasing scope of their projects/programs with limited resources and manpower.
  • clear expectations of project/program outcomes as well as limited support from their immediate supervisors to complete their work plans/goals
  • overwhelming workloads and needing more skills and training (technical, organizational, youth, etc.)
  • keeping youth engaged and motivated throughout the course of their projects/programs, while maintaining and maximizing youth participation and attendance.

In spite of these challenges, several CTC VISTAs reported success in combining their work with both new digital media and youth development:

  • CTC VISTA Cara Powers (from United Teen Equality Center (UTEC)) launched a weblog and posted information about her ideas on developing a youth multimedia magazine program. The mission/goal was "to empower youth through creative exploration of multimedia technology as a means of communication and transmission of ideas and information." and "to facilitate a media canvas that will serve as a multifaceted approach to communicating information and ideas in an interactive and engaging way.”
  • Taylor Hayles implemented a digital audio project/hip-hop club for the Neighborhood Technology Resource Center in Chicago that contributed to the rise in the self-esteem of the program participants.
  • Alli Shaughnessy started a started a summer Youth Media Club and assisted Boston Neighborhood Network staff with a girls youth production class, Girl TV, taught a group of six young women ages 13-16 field and studio production. The result of their work was a weekly BNNLive studio program where the girls will represent the local youth-serving organizations on their program.
  • CTC VISTA Nikki Payne visited digital media programs in Ecuador, Argentina and Chile and returned with curriculum and digital media best practices for distribution to other community technology centers in Houston (Technology For All) .

All in all , CTC VISTAs have been able to implement successful youth-centered digital media-related projects and programs that helped their designated sites maximize the power of technology to transform individuals and communities.
