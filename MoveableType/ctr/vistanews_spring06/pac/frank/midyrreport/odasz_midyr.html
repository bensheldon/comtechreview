CTC VISTAs in Community Networking

by Frank Odasz

The CTC VISTAs assigned to the priority area of Community Networking priority deal directly with empowering low-income Americans through the smart adoption of Internet knowledge-access, collaboration, and self-publishing tools and skills. The quality of technical assistance CTC VISTAs bring to their client communities is unprecedented and represents cutting edge demonstrations of what needs to be happen in all our communities.

These Community Networking projects include dramatically effective computer recycling programs, new consortia for sharing digital video across community media projects, large scale community computer and Internet training programs, historical archiving and storytelling on the web, new community collaboration software systems, cost-effective wireless home access, and more. The number of sophisticated community sites developed with a range of integrated customized and interactive capacities is astonishing.

Here are some highlights from their mid-year reports:

  • Marcus A. Foster Educational Institute/ OTXWest, Oakland, CA: Since 1995 OTXWest has distributed almost 15,000 computers to Oakland Public school students, families, classrooms, staff, and community access labs across the city.  OTXWest has diverted over 700 tons of e-waste from landfill to productive uses or responsible recycling and produced a manual and video on creating computer recycling programs. Since starting last fall, CTC VISTA Jeff Benton has completed construction of OTXWest's multimedia lab and is completing its digital storytelling curriculum. He is an integral part of all aspects of the home computer program, coordinating over 500 families in classes and the work of over 1,000 adult and youth volunteers.
  • Pangea Foundation, San Diego, CA: Pangea Foundation’s partnership with the CTC VISTA Project has continued to expand the Abilities-4-Resident Service Coordinators (A4RSC) program with three VISTAs this year: Mike Denegal, Daniel Richardson, and Sandra Hike. According to Supervisor Kristin Berry, "We currently have approximately 500 properties and 350 Service Coordinators for the A4RSC system nationwide serving 50,000 residents in low-income housing. This project was started by two CTCNet VISTAs at Pangea four years ago!" VISTAs are assisting with a mentoring program  providing technical assistance to six small community-based and faith-based organizations in San Diego and are setting up two school-based mentoring programs for at-risk and disabled youth, planned to expand to three schools next year.
  • Neighborhood Technology Resource Center, Chicago, IL: CTC VISTA Taylor Hales is helping NTRC achieve its mission by creating multi-media curricula and training programs that engage youth with technology and new media. He has introduced many lab users to new technologies and worked to create curricula that is age-appropriate and relevant to the audience. Supervisor Jillian Spindle says of Taylor that "his skills are unmatched in our organization with regard to multi-media development."
  • Lowell Telecommunications Corporation (LTC), Lowell, MA: LTC's mission is to serve the community with a platform for free speech and open communication. In that vein, the DigitalBicycle Project plays a key role as a distribution mechanism for LTC's member base and for multimedia makers everywhere. In the cable access world, if a producer wants to have a production shown in outside communities, taped videos need to be mailed or "bicycled."  DigitalBicycle Project is designed to create a quick and easy-to-use electronic distribution mechanism. Ben Sheldon, LTC's VISTA and "Online Community Developer," has been busy working on the construction and open beta testing of the DigitalBicycle and has designed its web site. Ben has also been working to build a web site to plot every community media center in the country on a web-based map called Mapping Access, http://mappingaccess.com/, that currently contains data for over 900 PEG access television stations.
  • Metropolitan Austin Interactive Network (MAIN)/TCRC, Bastrop/Austin, TX: CTC VISTA Nick Lewis helped TRRC overhaul online operations, converting some key servers and sites to new automated Content Management Systems (CMS), integrating Drupal and Civicspace. He also helped develop www.telecommunity.us (a new national community ICT resource project) and online resource for citizens of Austin to share written reports of community and individual life (working with the mayer and the Austin History Center in the public library) WriteOn, Austin!
  • CTCNet Chicago Chapter, Chicago, IL: Supervisor Michael Maranda writes: “We have been on a breakneck pace and I rely upon Dave Chakrabarti for a great deal. CTCNet Chicago has convened several major statewide events: the Digital Literacy Showcase, the Digital Neighborhoods Fundraiser, the Statewide Conference, and the launch of the statewide coalition on Community Tech Day. We've formulated the database staretgy and a sustainability plan. We've made an impact on the vision for the state community technology sector and launched a ring of websites in addition to the relaunch of our own site on a content managment system. This makes the generation of content much easier and more easily shared. Additionally we've put other webtools and communications infrastructure in place: phplist for our ilctc Bulletin, webforms for feedback, and hylafax for blast communications. Dave has specific experience in website design and search engine optimization techniques which has made progress in support of our website much faster. He's allowed us to help some connected organizations to advance their web strategy, building the reputation of our organization in the process.”
  • TINCAN (The Inland Northwest Community Access Network), Spokane, WA: Second Year CTC VISTA Nora Lollar is the Director of TINCAN's Inland Northwest Memories Project (http://history.tincan.org). Nora reports: "We have had a great time this year collecting history for the Inland Northwest memories project. University students from Gonzaga and Whitworth have been filling the Inland Northwest Memories Project’s online museum with local veterans' oral histories, photographs, and other Northwest history. We’ve teamed up with area high school students and artists to create historical murals on Spokane’s Garland District buildings. Local middle school students have worked during the past four months to collect and organize their local school district history that will go into a new museum in the newly established city of Spokane Valley." (Supervisor Karen Michaelson's report on Nora's work appeared in the last Community Technology Review and check out Nora's article in this issue of the newsletter.)

In addition to these great projects, we welcome new Community Networking VISTAs Ross Mussellman and Patrick Otton. Ross is serving with the Champaign-Urbana Community Wireless Network in IL (CUWiN) while Patrick is with the Madison Park Development Corporation in Roxbury/Boston.
